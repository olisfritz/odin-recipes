Gyudon Recipe

Description

Gyudon is one of the most famous "fast food" in Japan. Although this is considered as "cheap", don't skip out on this bowl, as this is packed full of flavour. By learning to make this dish, you are essentially learning the foundations of Japanese cuisine. This recipe will give you 3-4 servings.

Ingredients

  • 200-380g thinly sliced beef
  • 1/2 small-medium sized onion
  • 1/2 cup dashi
  • 1 tbsp sake
  • 2 tbsp mirin
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 sliced green onion
  • Japanese rice
  • Benishoga (picked ginger) - Optional

Steps

  1. In a medium sized pan, mix together dashi, sake, mirin, sugar, and soy sauce and heat up the pan to medium heat.
  2. Once the sugar dissolves and the sauce starts bubbling, add the onions.
  3. Once onions are translucent, turn down the leat slightly and add the beef.
  4. Let the beef cook and immediately stop when the meat turns pale pink. The residual heat should cook the meat fully. This prevents over-cooking of the meat.
  5. To assemble, get a medium sized bowl and add a bed of rice. Add in the beef (just enough to cover the rice) and top it off with a sprinkle of green onion and benishoga (optional).
  6. Serve and enjoy!! You can also add in a poached egg if you like!!
